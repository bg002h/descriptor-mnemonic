## Important
- **[I1″] The W production `s:Bx over single-node Bdu leaves` (spec :227) generates TYPE-INVALID miniscript for every non-`o` Bdu leaf — `s:` requires `Bo` (one input), and `pk_h`/`multi`/`multi_a` take ≥2 inputs.** Proven against pinned 13.0.0: `wsh(and_b(pk(A),s:pkh(B)))`, `wsh(and_b(pk(A),s:multi(1,B,C)))`, `tr(NUMS,and_b(pk(A),s:multi_a(1,B,C)))`, and `wsh(thresh(2,pk(A),s:pkh(B),s:pk(C)))` ALL fail typecheck: `attempts to use SWAP to prefix something which does not take exactly one input`. Only `pk(@i)` is `o` among the Bdu leaves; `a:` works over all of them (`a:pkh`, `a:multi`, `a:multi_a` all Ok, proven). Since W feeds both `and_b(B, W)` and `thresh(k, Bdu, W…)`, the generator as spec'd emits failing arms wholesale, and P6 step 1 explicitly forbids filtering ("a failure is a generator bug or a codec bug, both RED") → permanently red on a large generated fraction. Root cause traces to round-1's over-general evidence claim "both `s:` and `a:` over du-leaves accepted" (evidently tested only over `pk`), and the round-2 context-split fold makes it newly acute in tap (the keys-only tap Bdu is 2/3 non-`o`: `pk_h`, `multi_a`). Fix (one line): W = `s:pk(@i)` (the only `o` Bdu leaf) and `a:<any single-node Bdu leaf>`.
